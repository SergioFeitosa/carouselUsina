Broom Sorghum: A Plant with Multiple Uses

Broom sorghum, also known as broom sorghum or witches' broom, is a plant native to Africa and Europe. Asia which adapted very well to the Brazilian climate.
With its rusticity, resistance to drought and adaptability to different types of soil, broom sorghum has become one plant of great economic and social importance, especially in the most arid regions of the country.

Much More Than Brooms:

Although the popular name is broom sorghum, this plant offers several other uses, such as:

Animal feed: Broom sorghum is an important source of forage for livestock, providing nutrients and energy for animals.
Biomass: The plant can be used in the production of biomass to generate renewable energy, such as biogas and biofuels.
Civil construction: The broom sorghum stalk can be used in the manufacture of ecological bricks and panels of seal.
Crafts: The plant's fibers can be used in the production of baskets, hats, bags and others objects handcrafted.

Environmental Benefits:

The cultivation of broom sorghum brings several benefits to the environment, such as:

Erosion control: The plant's deep root system helps protect the soil against erosion, especially in sloped areas.
Improvement of soil fertility:The decomposition of organic matter in sorghum broom contributes to increase soil fertility, making it richer in nutrients.
Reduction in the use of pesticides: Broom sorghum is considered a plant resistant to pests and diseases, what reduces the need to use pesticides in its production.
Carbon capture:The plant helps capture and store carbon from the atmosphere, contributing to the combat the climate changes.

A Plant with Great Potential:

Broom sorghum is a plant with great potential for Brazil, especially in regions with a dry climate and semiarid.
With its diverse uses and benefits, this plant can contribute to socioeconomic development sustainable, generating income for rural communities and protecting the environment.
Invest in research and development, in the dissemination of its properties and in adding value to its products are some of the actions that can further boost the use of broom sorghum in Brazil and contribute to a future greener and more sustainable.
Broom sorghum is much more than just a plant. It is a source of opportunities, benefits and sustainability to Brazil.
