What is ICUMSA 600-1200 sugar?

The term “ICUMSA” means “International Commission for Uniform Methods of Sugar Analysis”. It is an organization that establishes standards for the analysis of sugars.

The number “600-1200” refers to the range of sugar color values, measured in ICUMSA units. This color scale is used to classify the color of refined white sugar.

ICUMSA 600-1200 Sugar is considered a darker colored sugar compared to standard refined sugar (generally with lower ICUMSA values).

It can be used in various applications, such as in the food and beverage industry, in candy manufacturing, in baking and in confectionery products.

The darker color may be due to impurities or the refining process.

Note: The color of sugar not only affects appearance, but can also influence the flavor and texture of the final products.
