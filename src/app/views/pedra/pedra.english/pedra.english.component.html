More than Beauty:

Gemstones are more than objects of beauty.
Throughout history, they have been used for various purposes, such as:

Amulets and talismans: Gemstones were believed to possess powers of healing, protection and luck.
Symbols of status and wealth: Precious stones were used to adorn crowns, jewelry and other objects of value, demonstrating power and wealth.
Currency of exchange: In some cultures, precious stones were used as currency of exchange, due to the its value and rarity.
Objects of art and decoration: Precious stones were carved and cut to create works of art and decorate objects, such as furniture and utensils.

A Global Market:

The precious stones market is one of the most important in the world, moving billions of dollars annually.
The main sources of precious stones are located in different countries, such as Brazil, South Africa, Colombia, Sri Lanka and Australia.

Cutting is an artisanal process that reveals the beauty and shine of precious stones.
Through precise techniques, cutters transform rough stones into jewelry sparkling, using tools such as magnifying glasses, polishers and diamond discs.

Care and Conservation:

Gemstones are valuable assets that require special care to maintain their beauty and shine.
It is important to protect them from falls, shocks, chemicals and sources of excessive heat.
Regular cleaning with warm water and neutral soap is essential to preserve the quality of the stones.
