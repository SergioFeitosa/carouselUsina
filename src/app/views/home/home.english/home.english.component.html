Sales Modus Operandi

In international trade, the terms of negotiation define the responsibilities and costs of the transportation of merchandise from seller to buyer.
Understanding these terms is crucial to ensuring a safe and efficient transaction, avoiding misunderstandings and additional costs.
In this guide, we willanalyze in detail the terms CIF (Cost, Insurance and Freight), FOB Stowed and FOB About Wheels, revealing their characteristics and differences.

1. CIF (Cost, Insurance and Shipping):

In CIF, the seller assumes responsibility for:

  • Cost of merchandise: The agreed price for the product itself.
  • Insurance: Purchase and payment of marine insurance to cover risks during transport.
  • Freight: Payment for transporting the goods to the port of destination designated in the contract.

The buyer assumes responsibility for:

  • Port expenses at the port of destination: Unloading fees, storage and other costs related to the arrival of goods at the port.
  • Internal transport: Contracting and paying for the transport of goods from the port of destination to O final location desired by the buyer.
  • Import expenses: Taxes, fees and other charges related to the entry of goods at the destination country.

2. FOB Stowed:

At FOB Estivado, the seller assumes responsibility for:

  • Cost of merchandise: The agreed price for the product itself.
  • Stevedoring: Placing the goods on board the ship, including costs for stacking, mooring and fixation.

The buyer assumes responsibility for:

  • Insurance: Purchase and payment of marine insurance to cover risks during transport.
  • Freight: Payment for the transportation of goods from the moment of stowage to the port of destiny designated in the contract.
  • Port expenses at the port of destination: Unloading fees, storage and other costs related to the arrival of goods at the port.
  • Internal transport: Contracting and paying for the transport of goods from the port of destination to O final location desired by the buyer.
  • Import expenses: Taxes, fees and other charges related to the entry of goods at the destiny country.

3. FOB On Wheels:

At FOB Sobre Rodas, the seller assumes responsibility for:

  • Cost of merchandise: The agreed price for the product itself.
  • Delivery of the goods to the buyer's vehicle or to a location designated by the buyer: seller assumes transportation costs to the delivery location.

The buyer assumes responsibility for:

  • Insurance: Hiring and paying for insurance (optional, depending on the agreement between the parties).
  • Import expenses: Taxes, fees and other charges related to the entry of goods at the destination country (if applicable).

Choosing the ideal trading term depends on several factors, like:

  • Type of merchandise: CIF is more used for maritime cargo, while FOB Estivated and FOB on Wheels can be used for different types of transport.
  • Location of the parties: The distance between the seller and the buyer influences the choice of more appropriate term to share costs and responsibilities.
  • Experience of the parties: If one of the parties does not have experiencewith import or export, the CIF can be more advantageous as it includes insurance.
  • Negotiation between the parties: Terms can be flexible and adjusted according to needs and preferences of both parties.
