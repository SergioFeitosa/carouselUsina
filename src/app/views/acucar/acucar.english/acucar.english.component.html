Sugar


What is Sugar?

Sugar is a simple carbohydrate, obtained mainly from sugar cane and sugar beet. It is an essential ingredient in several recipes and used to sweeten foods and drinks.

Types of Sugar:

Refined sugar: (ICUMSA 45) It goes through a refinement process that removes impurities and leaves the whiter and finer crystals.
Crystal sugar: (ICUMSA 150) It is the most common type, with large, dry crystals.
Brown sugar: (ICUMSA 600-1200) It is a less processed sugar, with moist crystals and a more flavorful flavor. intense.
Demerara sugar: (ICUMSA VHP) It is a type of brown sugar with larger crystals and a milder flavor.

Benefits of Sugar:

Energy: Sugar provides quick energy for the body, being important for physical activities and mental.
Flavour: Sweetens foods and drinks, making them tastier and more attractive.
Preservation: Helps preserve foods, such as fruit in jams.
Texture: In recipes, sugar contributes to the texture and creaminess of cakes, pies and other sweets.

Consumption Precautions:

Excessive sugar consumption can cause several health problems, such as obesity, diabetes, heart disease and tooth decay. It is important to consume sugar in moderation and within a diet. balanced.

Alternatives to Sugar:

To reduce sugar consumption, there are several alternatives, such as:
Natural sweeteners: stevia, xylitol, erythritol.
Fruits: banana, apple, grape.
Honey: natural sweetener with beneficial properties.

Conclusion:

Sugar is a versatile ingredient with many benefits, but it is important to consume it with moderation. Choosing alternatives to sugar and a balanced diet are important to maintain a good health.
