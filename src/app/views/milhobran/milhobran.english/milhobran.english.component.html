Corn bran



What is Corn Bran?

Derived from grinding corn grains, the outer part of the grain being rich in fiber
Natural source of insoluble and soluble fiber, important for digestive health
Rich in nutrients such as B vitamins, iron, magnesium and zinc
Low glycemic index, helping to control blood sugar levels
Gluten-free, making it a suitable option for celiacs and people with gluten sensitivity

Health Benefits of Corn Bran

Promotes digestive health: The high fiber content of corn bran contributes to good bowel function, preventing constipation and promoting regular bowel movements.
Helps with weight control: The fiber in corn bran increases satiety, helping to control appetite and reduce caloric intake, which can be beneficial for those looking to lose weight or maintain a healthy weight.
Regulates cholesterol levels: The soluble fiber present in corn bran can help reduce LDL (bad) cholesterol, reducing the risk of heart disease.
Controls blood sugar levels: The low glycemic index of corn bran helps control blood sugar levels, making it a beneficial option for people with diabetes or pre-diabetes.
Strengthens the immune system: Corn bran is rich in beta-glucan, a type of fiber that helps strengthen the immune system, fighting infections and diseases.

Culinary Uses of Corn Bran

Breakfast cereal: Add corn bran to your breakfast cereal to increase the fiber and nutrient content.
Yogurt and smoothies: Mix cornmeal into your yogurt or smoothies for an extra boost of fiber and flavor.
Muffins, Cookies and Breads: Replace some of the wheat flour with corn bran in your muffins, cookies and bread recipes to add texture, fiber and nutrients.
Burgers and meatballs: Incorporate corn bran into burgers and meatballs
