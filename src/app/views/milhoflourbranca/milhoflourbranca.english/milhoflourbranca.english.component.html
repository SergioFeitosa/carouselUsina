White Corn Flour



White Corn Flour: Flavor and Versatility at the Brazilian Table

White corn flour, a fundamental ingredient in Brazilian cuisine, is derived from corn kernels white and offers diverse possibilities of use, from traditional dishes to creative recipes.

A Little History:

The history of white corn flour in Brazil dates back to the time of indigenous peoples, who already used corn ground to prepare different foods.
With the arrival of Portuguese colonizers, the consumption of corn flour intensified, becoming a staple food in the population's diet.

Nutritional Properties:

White corn flour is a food rich in nutrients, such as:

Carbohydrates: Source of energy for the body.
Proteins: Important for the development and repair of muscle tissues.
Fibers: They help with digestion, regulate the intestine and promote satiety.
Vitamins: Rich in B vitamins, such as B1, B2 and B3, important for metabolism energy, the nervous system and muscular function.
Minerals: Source of iron, phosphorus, magnesium and potassium, essential minerals for the proper functioning of the body.

Health Benefits:

Regular consumption of white corn flour can bring several health benefits, such as:

Prevent chronic diseases: Helps in the prevention of chronic diseases such as type 2 diabetes, heart disease and some types of cancer.
Control cholesterol: The fiber present in corn flour helps reduce LDL cholesterol ("bad"), reducing the risk of heart disease.
Improve digestion:Fibers also help regulate the intestines and prevent constipation.
Strengthen the immune system: Rich in vitamins and minerals, corn flour helps to strengthen the immune system, protecting the body against diseases.
Promote satiety: The fibers and proteins in corn flour promote satiety, helping to body weight control.

Cooking Versatility:

White corn flour is an extremely versatile ingredient and can be used in a variety of dishes. Brazilian cuisine, such as:

Cornmeal cake: A traditional Brazilian cake made with white corn flour, milk, eggs, sugar and oil.
Cornmeal bread: A rustic bread made with white corn flour, milk, yeast and salt.
Couscous: A northeastern dish made with white corn flour, water and salt.
Angu: A dish from Minas Gerais made with white corn flour, water and salt.
Tamale: A typical Midwestern dish made with fresh grated corn, milk, sugar and salt.

White corn flour is a tasty, nutritious and versatile food that should make part of the regular diet of all Brazilians.
In addition to being a traditional and culturally important ingredient, white corn flour offers several health benefits and can be used in a variety of delicious dishes.
