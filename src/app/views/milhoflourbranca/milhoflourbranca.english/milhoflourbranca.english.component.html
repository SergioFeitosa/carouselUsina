White Corn Flour



White Corn Flour: Know Its Particularities

Produced from finely ground dry white corn kernels
Naturally gluten-free, making it a great option for celiacs and people with gluten sensitivity
It has a slightly sweet and mild flavor compared to yellow corn flour. Available in fine, medium and coarse grinds to suit different recipes.

White corn flour, also known as white cornmeal, is derived from finely grinding dried white corn kernels.
An essential feature of white cornmeal is that it is naturally gluten-free.
This makes it a perfect choice for celiacs and people with gluten sensitivity, who can enjoy delicious dishes without any worries.
White cornmeal has a slightly sweet and mild flavor compared to yellow cornmeal, making it ideal for certain recipes.
Furthermore, it is available in fine, medium and coarse grinds, meeting different culinary needs.
The fine grind is perfect for dishes like cakes and breads, while the medium and coarse grind are ideal for polenta and tortillas.

Culinary Delights with White Corn Flour

Breads, cakes and cookies: White corn flour adds a special touch to breads, cakes and cookies, leaving them soft, fluffy and with a slightly sweet flavor.
Crepes and pancakes: Try replacing wheat flour with white corn flour in your crepes and pancake recipes. The end result is light, soft and has a different flavor.
Tortillas and arepas: White corn flour is essential in Latin American cuisine for making tortillas and arepas.
Creamy Polenta: Prepare a delicious, creamy polenta using medium ground white cornmeal. You can serve it as a side dish or base for more elaborate dishes.
Soups and Stews: Finely ground white cornmeal can be used as a natural thickener for soups and stews, adding flavor and creaminess.
