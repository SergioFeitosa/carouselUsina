Corn Flour



What is Corn Flour?

Derived from finely ground dried corn kernels
Obtained from different varieties of corn, such as white, yellow and blue
Available in coarse, medium and fine grinds for different uses
Naturally gluten-free, making it a great alternative for people with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity

Types of Corn Flour

Yellow Corn Flour: This is the most common type, boasting a mild flavor and light yellow color. It's a workhorse in the kitchen, perfect for baking, cornbread, and polenta.
White Corn Flour: This variety offers a slightly sweeter and milder flavor compared to yellow corn flour. It stands out for making tortillas, arepas and is a great thickener for soups and stews.
Blue Corn Flour: Stand out from the crowd with blue corn flour! It has a unique nutty flavor and vibrant blue color that adds a stunning visual element to your dishes. Blue cornmeal shines in tortillas, enchiladas and can add a colorful pop to baked goods.

Culinary Magic of Corn Flour

Baking: Cornmeal adds a light, fluffy texture to pancakes, muffins, cookies and cakes.
Thicken: Use corn flour as a gluten-free thickener for soups, stews and sauces.
Ethnic Dishes: Basic ingredient of Latin American cuisine for tortillas, arepas, tamales and empanadas.
Frying: Coat seafood or vegetables with cornmeal for a light, crispy fried finish.
Polenta: Coarse cornmeal is cooked into a creamy polenta porridge, a versatile base for savory dishes.
