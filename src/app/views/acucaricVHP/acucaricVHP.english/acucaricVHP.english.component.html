What is VHP sugar?

The acronym “VHP” stands for “Very High Polarization”. This type of sugar is known for its high concentration of sucrose.

VHP sugar is produced from sugar cane juice and goes through an evaporation and crystallization process.

It is called “VHP” due to its high polarization, which refers to the amount of sucrose present in the sugar.

VHP sugar is generally used in the export industry, where it is transported in large quantities to other countries.

Its high concentration of sucrose makes it ideal for use in industrial processes, such as the manufacture of beverages, foods and chemicals.

Note: VHP sugar is unrefined and may contain impurities, but its high concentration of sucrose makes it valuable for many applications.
