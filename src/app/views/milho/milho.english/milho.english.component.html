Corn

Sweet Corn: A Taste of Summer in Every Grain

Sweet corn, also known as green corn, is a popular variety appreciated for its sweet flavor and juicy texture. Its grains are soft and creamy, perfect for eating fresh, boiled or roasted. Sweet corn is rich in vitamins, minerals and fiber, making it a nutritious addition to any meal. It is a versatile ingredient in cooking, used in dishes such as salads, soups, pies and even ice cream.

Popcorn Corn: Exploding with Flavor and Joy

Popcorn, the star of cinemas and parties, enchants with its magical pops and irresistible flavor. Its hard grains transform into soft and crunchy flakes when heated, providing a unique sensory experience. Popcorn corn is a source of fiber and antioxidants, as well as being a light, low-calorie snack. Savoring it pure, with butter, salt or other seasonings, is a pleasure that pleases all palates.

White Corn: Versatility for Everyday Life

White corn, a basic ingredient in many cultures, is known for its culinary versatility. Its soft, white grains are used in a variety of dishes, from flours and cookies to breads and tortillas. White corn is also ground to produce hominy, a popular food in Brazilian cuisine. Rich in starch, white corn is a natural thickener used in sauces, soups and puddings.

Purple Corn: An Explosion of Color and Nutrition

Purple corn, a superfood with exceptional antioxidant properties, stands out for its vibrant color and unique flavor. Its grains rich in anthocyanins, natural pigments, give purple corn a higher antioxidant power than other types of corn. Purple corn is used in various dishes, such as salads, pies and even drinks, such as juices and teas. In addition to its nutritional value, purple corn contributes to a colorful and aesthetically pleasing diet.
